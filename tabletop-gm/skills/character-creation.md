# Character Creation (SRD 5.1)

## Step-by-Step Overview

1. Choose a **Race**
2. Choose a **Class**
3. Determine **Ability Scores**
4. Choose a **Background**
5. Select **Equipment**
6. Calculate **Derived Stats** (AC, HP, saves, skills)
7. Add **Finishing Details** (name, appearance, personality, bonds, flaws)

---

## Step 1 — Race

Each race grants ability score bonuses, traits, and sometimes proficiencies or spells.

| Race | Ability Bonus | Key Traits |
|---|---|---|
| **Human** | +1 to all six ability scores | Versatile; extra language; no special resistances |
| **Elf (High)** | +2 DEX, +1 INT | Darkvision; keen senses; trance (4 hr rest); immunity to sleep magic; one wizard cantrip |
| **Elf (Wood)** | +2 DEX, +1 WIS | Darkvision; trance; Fleet of Foot (35 ft speed); Mask of the Wild |
| **Elf (Dark/Drow)** | +2 DEX, +1 CHA | Superior darkvision; sunlight sensitivity; innate spells (faerie fire, darkness) |
| **Dwarf (Hill)** | +2 CON, +1 WIS | Darkvision; resistance to poison; stonecunning; +1 HP per level |
| **Dwarf (Mountain)** | +2 CON, +2 STR | Darkvision; resistance to poison; stonecunning; medium armor proficiency |
| **Halfling (Lightfoot)** | +2 DEX, +1 CHA | Lucky (reroll 1s on d20); Brave; Nimble; Naturally Stealthy |
| **Halfling (Stout)** | +2 DEX, +1 CON | Lucky; Brave; Nimble; resistance to poison |
| **Gnome (Forest)** | +2 INT, +1 DEX | Darkvision; Gnome Cunning (advantage on INT/WIS/CHA saves vs magic); speak with small animals |
| **Gnome (Rock)** | +2 INT, +1 CON | Darkvision; Gnome Cunning; artificer's lore; tinker (construct tiny clockwork devices) |
| **Half-Elf** | +2 CHA, +1 to two others | Darkvision; Fey Ancestry; skill versatility (two extra skill proficiencies) |
| **Half-Orc** | +2 STR, +1 CON | Darkvision; Menacing (Intimidation proficiency); Relentless Endurance; Savage Attacks |
| **Dragonborn** | +2 STR, +1 CHA | Draconic ancestry (choose damage type); breath weapon; resistance to that damage type |
| **Tiefling** | +2 CHA, +1 INT | Darkvision; Hellish Resistance (fire); innate spells (thaumaturgy, hellish rebuke, darkness) |

## Step 3 — Ability Scores

Six abilities: **Strength (STR), Dexterity (DEX), Constitution (CON), Intelligence (INT), Wisdom (WIS), Charisma (CHA)**.

**Modifier:** `floor((score - 10) / 2)` — e.g., score 14 → +2, score 8 → -1.

### Method A: Standard Array

Assign these six values to any abilities: **15, 14, 13, 12, 10, 8**

Best for new players. Fast, balanced, no randomness.

### Method B: Point Buy

Start with all scores at 8. Spend **27 points** from the table below.

| Score | Cost |
|---|---|
| 8 | 0 |
| 9 | 1 |
| 10 | 2 |
| 11 | 3 |
| 12 | 4 |
| 13 | 5 |
| 14 | 7 |
| 15 | 9 |

Maximum score before racial bonuses: 15. Minimum: 8.

Best for players who want full control over their character concept.

### Method C: Roll 4d6, Drop Lowest

Roll 4d6, discard the lowest die, sum the remaining three. Repeat six times. Assign results to abilities freely.

Most dramatic — produces both powerful and flawed characters. Use with groups comfortable with variance.

## Step 4 — Background

Backgrounds grant two skill proficiencies, possibly tool or language proficiencies, and a narrative feature.

| Background | Skills | Extra Proficiencies | Feature |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Acolyte** | Insight, Religion | Two languages | Shelter of the Faithful |
| **Criminal** | Deception, Stealth | Gaming set, thieves' tools | Criminal Contact |
| **Folk Hero** | Animal Handling, Survival | Artisan's tools, vehicles (land) | Rustic Hospitality |
| **Noble** | History, Persuasion | Gaming set, one language | Position of Privilege |
| **Sage** | Arcana, History | Two languages | Researcher |
| **Soldier** | Athletics, Intimidation | Gaming set, vehicles (land) | Military Rank |
| **Hermit** | Medicine, Religion | Herbalism kit, one language | Discovery |
| **Outlander** | Athletics, Survival | Musical instrument, one language | Wanderer |
| **Charlatan** | Deception, Sleight of Hand | Disguise kit, forgery kit | False Identity |
| **Entertainer** | Acrobatics, Performance | Disguise kit, musical instrument | By Popular Demand |
| **Guild Artisan** | Insight, Persuasion | Artisan's tools, one language | Guild Membership |
| **Sailor** | Athletics, Perception | Navigator's tools, vehicles (water) | Ship's Passage |

Each background also includes **personality traits, an ideal, a bond, and a flaw** — pick or roll from tables, or invent your own.

## Step 6 — Derived Statistics

### Hit Points (HP)

- **Level 1:** Maximum hit die value + CON modifier
- Example: Fighter (d10) with CON 14 (+2) → 10 + 2 = **12 HP**

### Armor Class (AC)

| Armor Type | Formula |
|---|---|
| No armor | 10 + DEX modifier |
| Leather (light) | 11 + DEX modifier |
| Studded leather (light) | 12 + DEX modifier |
| Chain shirt (medium) | 13 + DEX modifier (max +2) |
| Scale mail (medium) | 14 + DEX modifier (max +2) |
| Breastplate (medium) | 14 + DEX modifier (max +2) |
| Half plate (medium) | 15 + DEX modifier (max +2) |
| Ring mail (heavy) | 14 (no DEX) |
| Chain mail (heavy) | 16 (no DEX; requires STR 13) |
| Splint (heavy) | 17 (no DEX; requires STR 15) |
| Plate (heavy) | 18 (no DEX; requires STR 15) |
| Shield | +2 AC (stacks with any armor) |
| Unarmored Defense (Barbarian) | 10 + DEX + CON |
| Unarmored Defense (Monk) | 10 + DEX + WIS |

### Saving Throws

Each class is proficient in **two saving throws** (listed on the class page). Proficient saves = ability modifier + proficiency bonus. Non-proficient saves = ability modifier only.

### Skill Proficiencies

Characters are proficient in skills from their class (choose from a list) and their background (fixed). Proficient skill check = ability modifier + proficiency bonus.

### Proficiency Bonus (Level 1): **+2**

## Tips for Helping New Players

- **Start with concept, not mechanics.** Ask: "What kind of character sounds fun to you?" Then map the concept to a class and race.
- **The standard array removes analysis paralysis.** Suggest it for first-time players.
- **Fighter is the best first class** — no resource management, intuitive actions, durable.
- **Cleric is secretly powerful** — heavy armor, healing, combat capability. Don't let new players underestimate it.
- **Avoid Monk at level 1** — Ki resource management is confusing until level 3.
- **Help them connect their background to their backstory** — one sentence about why they adventured is enough.
- **Don't sweat perfection** — a slightly suboptimal build played by an engaged player is more fun than an optimized one played reluctantly.
- **Remind them:** Everything can be changed before session 2. The first session is often the real test of fit.
