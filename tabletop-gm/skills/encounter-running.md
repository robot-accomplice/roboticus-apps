# Running Combat Encounters

## Starting Combat

Combat begins when a hostile action is taken or surprise is no longer a factor.

### Surprise

If any combatants are unaware at the start of combat (they were hidden, the party ambushed them,
or vice versa), those combatants are **surprised** during the first round. A surprised creature:
- Cannot move or take actions on their first turn
- Cannot take reactions until their first turn ends

Determine surprise before rolling initiative:
1. Compare the sneaking party's Stealth check against each target's passive Perception.
2. Any target whose passive Perception is lower than the Stealth check is surprised.

### Initiative

1. Call for Initiative: every player rolls **d20 + DEX modifier**.
2. Roll for each creature group (one roll per group is acceptable for large forces).
3. Arrange all combatants from highest to lowest result.
4. Ties: higher DEX score goes first; if still tied, the player decides or flip a coin.
5. Post the order at the top of your response and keep it visible throughout the encounter.

**Example initiative display:**
```
Initiative Order:
1. Kael (18)
2. Goblin Leader (15)
3. Mira (14)
4. Goblin Archers ×3 (11)
5. Borin (9)
6. Goblin Scouts ×2 (6)
```

## Round Structure

Each **round** represents approximately 6 seconds of in-world time. Every combatant acts once per round in initiative order.

---

## Player Turns

At the start of each player's turn, give them:
- Their current HP
- A brief tactical read of the situation
- Any relevant information their character would notice

**Formula:**
> "[Name], you're up! You have [X] HP. [Tactical note — where enemies are, who's down, any
> obvious hazard]. What do you do?"

**Action options (remind players as needed):**

| Action | Description |
|---|---|
| Attack | Make one or more weapon attacks (extra attacks at higher levels) |
| Cast a Spell | Cast a spell of 1 action casting time |
| Dash | Double your movement speed this turn |
| Disengage | Your movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks this turn |
| Dodge | Until your next turn, attacks against you have disadvantage; you have advantage on DEX saves |
| Help | Grant advantage to an ally's next attack or ability check |
| Hide | Make a Stealth check to become hidden |
| Ready | Declare an action and a trigger; the action fires when the trigger occurs |
| Search | Make a Perception or Investigation check to find something |
| Use Object | Interact with an object that requires more than a free interaction |

**Bonus actions** are granted by specific class features or spells — remind the player if they have one available.

**Free object interactions** (one per turn, no action required): draw/sheathe a weapon, open an unlocked door, pick up a dropped item, hand an item to another creature.

## Creature Turns

Run creature turns efficiently. Think in terms of their **instinct**:

1. **Move** toward their objective (close distance, reach cover, flank a target).
2. **Take their action** (attack most likely target, use a special ability if it applies).
3. **Use bonus actions** if available.

Narrate creature actions with flavor:
> "The goblin darts toward Borin, shortsword slashing upward — roll [X] to hit AC [Y]."

For groups of identical creatures acting together, resolve each attack individually but narrate them as a coordinated flurry.

**Creature morale (optional):** Intelligent creatures (INT 8+) may attempt to flee, surrender, or parley when reduced to 25% HP or fewer, or when their leader falls.

---

## Movement and Positioning

- Movement can be split before and after any action during a turn.
- Moving through difficult terrain costs double movement.
- Moving through another creature's space (not an enemy's) is allowed but counts as difficult terrain.
- Creatures can move into an enemy's space only as part of certain special actions.

**Opportunity Attacks:** When a creature moves out of an adjacent enemy's reach, that enemy may use its reaction to make one melee attack against the moving creature.

---

## Reactions

Each creature has **one reaction per round**, which resets at the start of their next turn.

Common reactions:
- **Opportunity Attack** — melee attack when a creature leaves your reach
- **Readied Action** — fires when your declared trigger occurs
- **Shield (spell)** — adds +5 to AC as a reaction to being hit
- **Counterspell** — interrupts a spell being cast

When a reaction-triggering event occurs, pause narration and ask: "Does anyone want to use a reaction here?"

---

## Tracking HP and Status

Keep a running count for each creature group. You do not need to reveal exact monster HP; instead use fictional language:

- "The orc is barely standing, blood streaming from a deep gash."
- "The troll looks unfazed — your swords barely drew ichor."

Players' HP is their own to track. Ask each player their HP before their turn.

**Status effects:** Note any active conditions (poisoned, prone, grappled, etc.) next to a creature's name in the initiative order.

---

## Ending Combat

Combat ends when:
- All hostile creatures are dead, incapacitated, or have fled
- The players escape or surrender
- A negotiated pause occurs (parley, surrender, dramatic revelation)

After combat:
1. Confirm which creatures are dead vs. unconscious/fleeing.
2. Give the party a moment to stabilize downed characters.
3. Describe the aftermath with sensory detail — smoke, silence, the smell of blood, the weight of what just happened.
4. Ask: "What do you do?" — let the players dictate the pacing.

**Experience and loot** are awarded at GM discretion at a natural narrative pause (not necessarily immediately after each fight).
