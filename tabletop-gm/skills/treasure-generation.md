# Treasure Generation (SRD 5.1)

## Coin Types and Values

| Coin | Abbreviation | Value in Gold |
|---|---|---|
| Copper piece | cp | 1/100 gp |
| Silver piece | sp | 1/10 gp |
| Electrum piece | ep | 1/2 gp |
| Gold piece | gp | 1 gp |
| Platinum piece | pp | 10 gp |

**Note:** Electrum pieces are rare and distrusted in many regions — many merchants won't accept them at face value. Use sparingly for flavor.

| CR | Copper | Silver | Electrum | Gold | Platinum |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

| 17+ | — | — | — | 2d6 | 8d6 |

Roll on the appropriate row and convert as needed. A CR 3 orc raider carries a handful of copper, perhaps a few silver. A CR 12 warlord carries meaningful gold.

## Treasure Hoards (by CR Threshold)

Hoards belong to significant locations, boss creatures, or accumulated wealth.

### CR 0–4 Hoard
- Coins: 6d6 × 100 cp, 3d6 × 100 sp, 2d6 × 10 gp
- Gems/Art: roll d6 — on 4–6, include 2d6 × 10 gp in gems or art objects (value ~10 gp each)
- Magic Items: roll d6 — on 6, one item from the Common or Uncommon table

### CR 5–10 Hoard
- Coins: 2d6 × 100 sp, 2d6 × 100 gp
- Gems/Art: 2d4 art objects or gems (value ~25–250 gp each)
- Magic Items: roll d6 — on 4–6, one Uncommon item; on 6, one Rare item

### CR 11–16 Hoard
- Coins: 4d6 × 100 gp, 1d6 × 10 pp
- Gems/Art: 3d6 gems (value ~250–750 gp each)
- Magic Items: 1d3 Rare items; roll d6 — on 5–6, one Very Rare item

### CR 17+ Hoard
- Coins: 12d6 × 1000 gp, 8d6 × 100 pp
- Gems/Art: 1d10 art objects or gems (value ~2500–7500 gp each)
- Magic Items: 1d4 Very Rare items; roll d6 — on 5–6, one Legendary item

## Mundane Treasure

Not all loot is coin. Trade goods and valuables add texture and can be more interesting than gold.

### Art Objects (example values)
- **10 gp:** A silver hairpin shaped like a dragonfly; a painted miniature portrait; a carved wooden music box
- **25 gp:** A carved bone statuette; a gold locket with a portrait inside; an ornate bronze mirror
- **250 gp:** A carved ivory statuette; a gold ring set with bloodstones; a tapestry depicting a historic battle
- **750 gp:** A silver chalice with inlaid niello; a carved harp of exotic wood with ivory pegs; a gilded ceremonial helm
- **2500 gp:** A gold idol with gemstone eyes; an ornate golden ring set with rubies; a platinum bracelet with sapphires

### Gems (example values)
- **10 gp:** Azurite, banded agate, eye agate, moss agate, obsidian, turquoise
- **50 gp:** Bloodstone, carnelian, chalcedony, jasper, moonstone, onyx, rock crystal, zircon
- **100 gp:** Amber, amethyst, chrysoberyl, coral, jade, jet, pearl, tourmaline
- **500 gp:** Alexandrite, aquamarine, violet garnet, black pearl, golden yellow topaz
- **1000 gp:** Emerald, white opal, black opal, fire opal, blue sapphire, star ruby, star sapphire
- **5000 gp:** Diamond, jacinth, ruby

### Trade Goods
Useful as transaction currency in areas where gold is scarce:
- Saffron (1 lb = 15 gp), cinnamon/pepper (2 gp/lb), silk (10 gp/lb)
- Iron (1 gp/lb), copper (5 sp/lb), salt (5 cp/lb), wheat (1 cp/lb)
- Livestock: chicken (2 cp), pig (3 gp), ox (15 gp), horse (75–400 gp)

| Rarity | Typical Level Range | Approx. Value |

### Common Items (SRD 5.1 Examples)
1. **Potion of Healing** — Restore 2d4+2 HP as a bonus action
2. **Cantrip Scroll** — Single-use scroll containing any cantrip
3. **Message** — A small quill that writes at the owner's verbal command
4. **Cloak of Billowing** — Billows dramatically on command (cosmetic)
5. **Hat of Wizardry** — While wearing, can use it as a spellcasting focus; roll d20 when casting a cantrip — on 1, cast a random cantrip instead

### Uncommon Items (SRD 5.1 Examples)
1. **Bag of Holding** — 500 lb / 64 cubic ft extradimensional interior
2. **Cloak of Protection** — +1 to AC and saving throws (requires attunement)
3. **Dust of Disappearance** — Packet of dust that turns a creature invisible for 2d4 minutes
4. **Goggles of Night** — Grants darkvision 60 ft
5. **Potion of Greater Healing** — Restore 4d4+4 HP
6. **Ring of Swimming** — Gain swimming speed equal to walking speed
7. **Rope of Climbing** — 60-ft animated rope, holds up to 3000 lb
8. **Sending Stones** — Pair of stones; use one to cast Sending to the holder of the other
9. **Spell Scroll (1st or 2nd level)** — Cast a specific spell once

### Rare Items (SRD 5.1 Examples)
1. **Belt of Giant Strength (Hill)** — STR becomes 21
2. **Boots of Speed** — Double speed, provoke opportunity attacks with disadvantage (bonus action, 10 min/day; requires attunement)
3. **Necklace of Fireballs** — Beads that can be thrown as Fireball (8d6 fire damage per bead)
4. **Ring of Protection** — +1 AC and saving throws (requires attunement)
5. **Staff of Healing** — 10 charges; cure wounds, lesser restoration, mass cure wounds (requires attunement by cleric, druid, or paladin)
6. **Wand of Lightning Bolts** — 7 charges; cast Lightning Bolt (8d6) per charge
7. **Winged Boots** — Fly speed equal to walking speed, 4 hours/day (requires attunement)

### Very Rare Items (SRD 5.1 Examples)
1. **Amulet of the Planes** — Attune to attempt to plane shift; failure sends you to a random plane (requires attunement)
2. **Belt of Giant Strength (Fire/Frost/Stone)** — STR becomes 25 (variant by giant type)
3. **Cloak of Displacement** — Attacks against you have disadvantage until you take damage each round (requires attunement)
4. **Manual of Bodily Health** — Read over 48 hours; CON permanently +2 (max 24); destroyed on use
5. **Ring of Regeneration** — Regain 1d6 HP every 10 min if at least 1 HP (requires attunement)
6. **Staff of Power** — 20 charges; multiple spells; +2 to attack, saves, AC; Retributive Strike on destruction (requires attunement by sorcerer, warlock, or wizard)
7. **Tome of Clear Thought** — Read over 48 hours; INT permanently +2 (max 24); destroyed on use

### Legendary Items (SRD 5.1 Examples)
1. **Apparatus of Kwalish** — 10-ft steel lobster-shaped submersible vehicle
2. **Belt of Giant Strength (Storm/Cloud)** — STR becomes 27 or 29
3. **Cloak of Invisibility** — Become invisible while wearing and with hood up; 2 hours/day in 1-min increments (requires attunement)
4. **Crystal Ball (True Seeing)** — Scry and see through illusions; always active within sensor (requires attunement)
5. **Ring of Wishes** — Contains up to 3 wishes; when all expended, becomes a nonmagical ring (requires attunement)
6. **Staff of the Magi** — 50 charges; vast spell list; Retributive Strike on destruction (requires attunement by sorcerer, warlock, or wizard)
7. **Vorpal Sword** — +3 to attack and damage; on a natural 20, sever the target's head if it has one (requires attunement)

## When and How to Distribute Treasure

### Pacing guidelines
- **Levels 1–4:** Primarily coin, occasional Common or Uncommon magic item as a major reward
- **Levels 5–10:** Mix of coin + gems/art + reliable Uncommon items, one Rare item per major arc
- **Levels 11–16:** Rare items are standard, Very Rare items mark campaign milestones
- **Levels 17–20:** Very Rare and Legendary items; wealth is measured in tens of thousands of gp

### Distribution methods
- **Roleplayed discovery:** Found in a specific, described location with narrative weight
- **Quest reward:** Named, negotiated before the quest — builds player investment
- **Incidental loot:** Carried by defeated enemies — keep it proportional to the creature's lifestyle
- **Merchant sale:** Let players spend accumulated gold; introduce interesting items for purchase

### Placement tips
- Put treasure where it makes sense in the fiction: a dragon's hoard is different from a bandit's purse
- Give magic items history: who made it, who last carried it, what it's been used for
- Let players find treasure that doesn't match their needs — trading, selling, or giving it away is its own gameplay

## Avoiding Monty Haul and Stingy GM Syndromes

### Monty Haul (too much)
Signs: Players are buying buildings at level 5, hoarding dozens of magic items, treating gold as meaningless.

Corrections:
- Scale prices and bribes up. A city gate guard wants 50 gp, not 5 gp.
- Introduce maintenance costs: ships, strongholds, followers, spell components
- Make selling magic items difficult — most merchants can't afford them; finding a buyer is a quest
- Use wealth as leverage: factions want a cut; tax collectors appear; thieves guilds demand tribute

### Stingy GM (too little)
Signs: Players are at level 6 with no magic items, constantly resource-starved, losing fights they should win.

Corrections:
- Review the SRD hoard tables and calibrate to them
- Hand out healing potions more freely — they reduce attrition without being powerful
- Remember that a Bag of Holding or Cloak of Protection is not game-breaking at level 5
- Reward creative play, not just combat wins

## Quest Rewards Beyond Treasure

Gold isn't always the right reward. These are often more memorable:

- **Titles and land:** "Sir Aldric of Thornfield" — carries weight in society, opens doors, creates obligations
- **Favors:** "The Archmage owes you one" — fungible in unexpected ways
- **Information:** The location of a hidden cache, the identity of a hidden enemy, the solution to a mystery
- **Contacts:** Access to a fence, a healer, a shipwright, a spy
- **Training:** Proficiency in a new skill, a language, a tool — flavored as earned instruction
- **Reputation:** Factions in the region remember what the party did; prices improve, guards look away, doors open
- **Unique items with story value:** Not powerful by stats, but significant by history — a general's medal, a queen's personal seal, a map to something greater
