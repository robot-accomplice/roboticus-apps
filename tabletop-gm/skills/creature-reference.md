## Creature Tactics by Intelligence

**INT 3 or lower (animal, mindless undead):**
Attacks the nearest visible target. Flees when injured and cornered. No tactics.

**INT 4–7 (goblin, zombie, most beasts):**
Basic pack instinct. Focuses on isolated targets. Retreats to regroup. Won't sacrifice for others.

**INT 8–10 (orc, hobgoblin, bandit):**
Sets ambushes. Focuses fire on the most dangerous target. Uses cover. Will flee when losing.
May parley for escape if clearly outmatched.

**INT 11–14 (knight, mage, cult fanatic):**
Prioritizes healers and spellcasters. Uses terrain advantage. Coordinates with allies.
Will negotiate, surrender with conditions, or sacrifice a pawn to escape.

**INT 15+ (vampire, dragon, archmage):**
Has a plan. Studies the party before engaging. Uses feints, manipulation, information warfare.
Treats combat as a last resort. Always has an escape plan.

**Steps:**

2. Sum the XP values of all monsters in the encounter.
3. Apply the **multiplier** based on number of monsters (see below).

## Improvising Creatures

When you need a creature that isn't in the SRD, use this framework:

1. **Pick a CR** that matches the threat level you want.
2. **Set AC** = 13 + (CR / 4), rounded down.
3. **Set HP** = CR × 15 (adjust ±30% for fragile/tough).
4. **Attack bonus** = +4 + (CR / 4), rounded down.
5. **Damage per round** = scale to the CR (CR 1: ~12 DPR, CR 5: ~35 DPR, CR 10: ~65 DPR).
6. Give it **one signature ability** that makes it distinct (a recharge breath, a grapple, a frightful presence, a regeneration).
7. Pick **one vulnerability** or **one immunity** to reward player preparation.

The goal is a creature that's dangerous for 2–4 rounds and memorable for one reason.
